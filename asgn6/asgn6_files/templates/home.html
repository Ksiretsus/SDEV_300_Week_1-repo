History

Based on the 1995 movie of the same name, Goldeneye is a first-person shooter that was launched in 1997. Origianlly planned to be released on the Super Nintendo, the finalized Goldeneye 64 product was wildly different from its initial pitch to Nintendo as a 2D platformer. The production studio Rareware gave the development team a huge swathe of creative freedom and the project evolved from a 2D platformer, to an on-rails shooter like Virtua Cop, and then to its final state as THE game that dragged first-person shooters from the smaller Doom, Quake, and Wolfenstein community and into the mainstream.

Development

The working vision of the team was to create a game that allowed players more freedom of movement, and more interaction with the environment than the previous genreation of shooters. They came up with many new ideas that hadn't been implemented before on consoles such as:

  1. Gun reloading
  2. Position dependent hits on enemies
  3. Bullet marks on walls
  4. Cartridge case ejections

and level design that had a lot of emphasis on interesting spaces, with some areas having no relevance to a level other than that of a place to explore. Probably the most important release feature of the game was its multiplayer mode. Multiplayer was completely unplanned, yet it was added just four months before the games release. Programmer Steve Ellies sat in his office and created most of the multiplayer mode, while other team members assisted after hours with Rareware and Nintendo not being aware of this side project until just before release. It turned out to be the part of the project that kept Goldeneye in the top 10 or 20 lists even today, 25 years after its release.

Impact

Goldeney brought the first-person shooter genre to the console market. Before that, FPS style games were limited to desktop computers and were largely single player. One of its biggest impacts was that of multiplayer. While many desktop shooters of the time had multiplayer modes, it tended to be cumbersome and needed at least two computers, networking equpment, two monitors, and the space for it all. In contrast the equipment for a four player Goldeneye match could be carried by one person. Goldeneye also brought the genre out of the 'Shoot everything' gameplay style of Doom and Quake, by introducing stealth and noise radius which rewarded players for being clever and avoiding guards. A final lasting feature of the game is the zoomable sniper rifle, which had not bee seen before but is now ubiquitous in the FPS genre.

Continuing Legacy:

Speedrunning

Goldeneye caught the interest of the speedrunning community due to the inclusion of an in game level timer. The speedrunning community is large and has gotten bigger proportionally to the interent. While most play the game as designed, speedrunners thrive on finding exploits that allow them to shave time off of levels with some being completed in unde rone minute.

Mod/Hack Community

The modding community creates tools that they use to modify the Goldeneye game code in order to create new multiplayer rule sets, modify single player difficulty settings, change AI behavior, and even create new full length games based on other James Bond movies, but using the Goldeneye game engine. All of this content is made freely available to everyone.
